Schema Designs

Schemas define how data is organized in a relational database for analytical purposes. Two popular designs are the Star Schema and the Snowflake Schema.

Star Schema

  • Central fact table surrounded by denormalized dimension tables.
  • Simpler and faster queries due to reduced joins.
  • Includes redundant data, but this is acceptable in analytical contexts.

Advantages:

  • Simple structure.
  • Optimized for query performance.

Snowflake Schema

  • Central fact table with normalized dimension tables.
  • Reduces redundancy by splitting dimensions into smaller tables.
  • Slower queries due to additional joins.

Advantages:

  • Less redundancy.
  • Suitable for complex datasets.
